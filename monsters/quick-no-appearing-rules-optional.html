Quick No. Appearing Rules (Optional)

White Box lacks the "No. Appearing" line for its monster stat blocks that many OSR games have. This page exists to provide a simple framework for determining how many monsters are encountered in a dungeon, in the wild, or in their own lair when planning out adventures or stocking dungeons. They are ecology-first guidelines: use the creature's encounter pattern and terrain before worrying about combat math.

How to Determine Number Appearing

  1. Pick the creature's encounter pattern from the Quick No. Appearing Table. If you are uncertain about the creature's ecology, use the Fallback by Threat or HD table instead.
  2. Choose Dungeon, Wild, or Lair, then roll the listed expression.
  3. For named monsters, special story encounters, or unusual local circumstances, override the result freely. The table is a fast baseline, not a constraint.

Context

Dungeon

Room, corridor, patrol, tomb, cave, ruin, or immediate encounter.

Wild

Travel or scouting encounter in the wider territory; usually only a little bigger than Dungeon.

Lair

Den, camp, hive, village, crypt, or home base. Count combat-capable creatures unless you want dependents too.

When Pattern Is Unclear

Use this fallback only when a monster's ecology is not obvious. Prefer the encounter-pattern table when the creature's behavior is clear.
